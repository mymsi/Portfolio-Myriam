Octobre Rose

Projet de Street Art

Concept

Every October there are campaigns to raise awareness of breast cancer and encourage people to go for screening. I imagined a campaign based on street art.
Several portraits of women would be dispatched in the city of Paris, linked together by a pink and greyscale colour code. The aim is to use images to show the reality of breast cancer, the women most affected by it and the different levels of intervention.
The image on the right is only a sample, showing the sometimes frightenning reality of a surgical operation.

Concept

The aim of the application is to help you manage your water consumption and find out more about the water you drink in general.
The principle of the game is similar to that of Tamagochi. In Tamagochi, you have to log in every day to feed and care for your Tamagochi, which is similar to an animal, with the aim of making it grow and keep it alive.
In our game, the Tamagochi is a garden that the child looks after. To do this, they have to log on every day to complete water-themed challenges. These challenges could take several forms: quizzes, craft workshops, mini-games and so on. There would be a maximum limit of two challenges per day. The challenges would focus on real-life situations (e.g. ‘Turn off the tap water when you brush your teeth’), to encourage children to draw parallels with their own lives and then regulate their own water consumption. Each challenge allows children to acquire new knowledge about water and earn points, which in turn can be used to buy fertiliser or new plants for their garden.
